A Deep History

Click each era to reveal its story

Pre-1600s
The Khoikhoi and San peoples of the Cape brewed wild honeybush as a daily tisane — its naturally sweet taste made it a staple across generations.
Pre-1600s
Khoikhoi origins
1700s
Dutch East India Company botanists documented honeybush at the Cape. VOC traders began exporting dried Cape herbs to European markets.
1700s
European contact
1820s
British settlers in the Eastern Cape adopted honeybush brewing from local communities. It became a household staple in the Langkloof and Tsitsikamma valleys.
1820s
Settler adoption
1990s–2000s
South African researchers developed standardised processing methods. The first commercial honeybush teas reached export markets, building global interest alongside rooibos.

Origin
Honeybush grows only in South Africa's Western and Eastern Cape — the Cape Floristic Region, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The ~23 Cyclopia species are found nowhere else on Earth, thriving in nutrient-poor, acidic Fynbos soils.

Traditional
For centuries the most common form. Dried, oxidised honeybush is steeped in hot water, producing an amber, naturally sweet infusion with no bitterness and no caffeine. No need to add milk or sugar.

Global reach
South Africa exports honeybush to Europe, Asia, and North America, where it appears in specialty tea shops and wellness brands. Alongside rooibos, it represents South Africa's most distinctive botanical heritage.

What exactly is honeybush? +
Honeybush is a group of flowering shrubs in the genus Cyclopia, belonging to the legume family. Around 23 species grow exclusively in South Africa's Cape Fynbos — they are found nowhere else on Earth. The plant is named for its sweet honey-like fragrance, which comes from the golden-yellow flowers that bloom in winter and spring.
Does honeybush contain caffeine? +
No. Honeybush is naturally caffeine-free — it contains no caffeine whatsoever. This is one of the reasons it has been enjoyed for generations as an everyday drink, at any time of day or night.

What does honeybush taste like? +
Honeybush has a naturally sweet, smooth taste with notes of warm honey, dried fruit, and a subtle floral warmth. Unlike most herbal teas, it has no bitterness — even when steeped for a long time. NARI adds peach and cherry blossom for a fruity, gently floral profile.

How is NARI different from rooibos? +
Both grow in South Africa's Cape region and are caffeine-free, but they are different plants. Rooibos (Aspalathus linearis) has an earthy, slightly woody flavour. Honeybush (Cyclopia) is distinctly sweeter, softer, and more floral — many people find it gentler to drink without any additions.
